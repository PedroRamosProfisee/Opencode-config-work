# Agent Communication Profile

**Purpose**: User-specific guidance for how agents should explain, teach, structure information, and deliver feedback.

**Last Updated**: 2026-04-25

---

## Assessment Snapshot

- **Felder-Silverman:** Active +7, Intuitive +1, Visual +5, Sequential +3
- **VARK:** Multimodal (Visual + Kinesthetic)
- **Mindset:** mixed/uncertain; setbacks and negative feedback can reduce confidence and challenge-seeking

---

## Communication Defaults

- Start with a concise summary, then provide clear next steps.
- Prefer numbered steps, bullets, tables, checklists, and comparisons over dense prose.
- Use visual structure when possible: matrices, diagrams, flow breakdowns, and before/after examples.
- Move quickly from explanation to application.
- Keep sequencing explicit and progressive.
- Pair practical examples with a brief explanation of why they work.

---

## Teaching Preferences

- Bias toward hands-on, build-first learning.
- Suggest a small implementation or exercise early.
- Prefer resources with examples, visuals, or interactive practice.
- Reinforce with retrieval practice, spaced repetition, dual coding, deliberate practice, and teaching back.

---

## Feedback Preferences

- Be supportive, direct, and specific.
- Critique the artifact or process, not the person.
- Convert criticism into next steps, experiments, or checklists.
- Avoid harsh or identity-based phrasing.

---

## Caveat

Treat these results as preference signals, not hard rules. Optimize for measurable outcomes over comfort alone.
