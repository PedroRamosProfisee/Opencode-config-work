# Step 06 — Market Viability

## Goal

Decide whether the idea has a reachable, painful, monetizable market before investing heavily in implementation.

## Default lens

Assume indie/SaaS:

- Small team or solo founder.
- Limited budget for paid acquisition.
- Founder-led sales/content/community are likely first channels.
- Validation should happen before large build.
- Prefer narrow paid pain over broad free interest.

## Branches to resolve

1. **Audience** — Who exactly has this pain?
2. **Pain severity** — How costly/annoying/risky is the problem?
3. **Frequency** — How often does the pain occur?
4. **Alternatives** — What are they already doing or paying for?
5. **Switch trigger** — What event makes them seek a new solution?
6. **Reachability** — Where can we find them repeatedly?
7. **Buyer control** — Can the person with pain buy without committee approval?
8. **Competitive density** — Crowded, fragmented, neglected, or emerging?
9. **Wedge** — What is the first narrow advantage?
10. **Evidence level** — Opinion, public evidence, interviews, waitlist, paid pilots, retained customers.
11. **Personal vs commercial success** — Is solving the creator/family/internal need enough even if the market is weak?

## Evidence ladder

Recommend the next evidence level to pursue:

0. Founder opinion only
1. Anecdotal comments
2. Public pain evidence
3. Direct interviews
4. Waitlist / qualified leads
5. Paid pilots / preorders
6. Retained paying customers
